CNNs

A Place to Explore Convolutional Neural Networks

Background

Convolutional Neural Networks are a class of deep learning algorithms primarily used for image recognition but have proven effective for NLP tasks like sentiment analysis.

They utilize layers that apply convolutions on word embeddings (dense vector representations of words that capture their semantic meaning) to capture contextual patterns in text, allowing them to discern complex features such as the sentiment expressed in sentences or documents. By leveraging their hierarchical feature extraction capabilities, CNNs can identify varying levels of sentiment intensity, making them powerful tools for analyzing emotions in content like movie reviews and social media posts. This adaptability showcases the versatility of CNNs in handling different types of data beyond their original domain.

Details:

  • Sequence Length: 256
  • 300 total Word Embeddings: Pretrained Stanford GloVe with filters of sizes 3, 5, and 7
  • Filter Stride: 1
  • Convolutional Layer Activation Function: ReLU
  • Pooling: Max Pooling
  • Probability Distribution: Softmax Function
  • Dropout Rate: 50%
  • Loss Function: Cross Entropy Loss
  • Optimizer: Adaptive Moment Estimation
