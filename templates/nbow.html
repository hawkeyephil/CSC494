NBoWs

A Place to Explore Neural Bag of Words

Background

The Neural Bag of Words model represents text as a bag of words and uses a neural network to learn word representations and make classifications

This approach considers text as an unordered collection of words. Each word is considered a feature. The number of times words appear in the text is identified. The entire text is represented as a vector of word frequencies. Neural bag of words models uses the bag of words as an input. The vectors are then used as inputs into either a forward neural network or recurrent neural network for classification. This approach is relatively simple yet effective. However, bag of words models ignore word order. This is a limiting factor which results in a loss of word context and can hinder attaining advanced levels of classification accuracy. For these reasons, neural bag of words models often act as a baseline to evaluate performance of more complex neural networks.
